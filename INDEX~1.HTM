Placement Success Advisor — Candidate Assignment

Estimated time: most candidates complete this assignment in 45–60 minutes.

Context

Imagine you've just joined Virtual Internships as a Placement Success Advisor (PSA).

Every day you'll balance competing priorities across a portfolio of internships. You'll coach interns, support host companies, manage difficult conversations, and decide where your time has the biggest impact.

This exercise is designed to simulate a typical week in the role. We're interested in how you think, not whether you arrive at the same conclusions we would.

Part 1 — Monday Morning

It's 9:00am on Monday. You open your laptop and have the following placements requiring your attention. You know you won't be able to action everything immediately.

Case A — Falling Behind Quietly
Week 8

Sarah is completing her internship for university credit. Her weekly timesheets show she's averaging 6 hours per week. Her university requires 10 hours per week to receive academic credit. Neither Sarah nor her supervisor has raised any concerns.

Case B — Relationship Deteriorating
Week 5

Yesterday a supervisor submitted the following feedback:

"The intern is technically capable, but communication has become inconsistent. Emails are taking days to answer and it's becoming difficult to rely on them."

The intern hasn't submitted this week's feedback.

Case C — Commitment Made

Last Friday you promised an intern you'd send guidance for completing their final reflection. The reflection is due tomorrow. This morning they emailed:

"Just checking whether you've had a chance to send this through."
Case D — Scheduled Review

You have a scheduled Week 5 review call at 10:00am. Neither the intern nor the supervisor has raised any concerns.

Your Tasks

1. Prioritisation

Rank the four cases in the order you would action them. For each case explain:

  • Why you've prioritised it there.
  • What your immediate action would be.
  • Which case(s), if any, you're comfortable delaying, and why.

2. You Have Time to Send One Email

Before you jump into calls, you have 10 minutes available. You have time to send one email before starting your day. Choose one of the following:

  • Option A — Reply to the supervisor from Case B.
  • Option B — Email Sarah from Case A.
  • Option C — Reply to the intern from Case C.

Write the email exactly as you would send it. There isn't one correct choice — we're interested in why you chose that stakeholder first and how you approach the conversation.

Part 2 — Wednesday Morning

It's now Wednesday. Here's what happened.

Case A

Sarah's Academic Advisor has contacted Virtual Internships asking whether she's still on track to receive academic credit. No one has yet spoken to Sarah about her hours.

Case B

The supervisor has emailed again requesting to speak with someone senior. They've said they're beginning to lose confidence in the placement.

Case C

The intern never received your guidance. Their university submission deadline is today.

Case D

Your scheduled Week 5 review went ahead. Everything was progressing well and no concerns were identified.

Your Written Response

Looking back:

  • Would you change your Monday prioritisation?
  • What would you do differently now?
  • Would you still choose to send the same email first? Why or why not?

Part 3 — Video Reflection

Maximum 4 minutes. Imagine you're debriefing this week with your manager. Talk us through your thinking from Monday to Wednesday. Please cover:

  • How you prioritised the four cases on Monday.
  • Why you chose to send that email before anything else.
  • What assumptions you made.
  • What changed after Wednesday's update.
  • Whether your priorities changed — and why.
  • If you were starting Monday again, what would you do differently?

Camera on or screen share is completely fine. No editing or polished presentation is expected — we're simply interested in understanding how you think through competing priorities.

Role Overview
What does a PSA do?
As a Placement Success Advisor (PSA), you play a central role in ensuring both interns and host companies achieve meaningful outcomes. You will guide interns through their learning journey, support host companies in achieving their goals, and proactively address challenges to ensure smooth, productive internships. This role allows you to see the direct impact of your work on professional growth, engagement, and successful programme completions.
Key Responsibilities
  • Guide the full internship journey — from onboarding to offboarding, including weekly progress tracking and evaluations.
  • Monitor performance and intervene proactively — identify potential challenges early and provide solutions that keep interns on track.
  • Build and maintain strong relationships — communicate clearly and consistently with both interns and host companies, fostering trust and collaboration.
  • Solve problems constructively — address mismatched expectations, skill gaps, or underperformance with actionable plans.
  • Ensure completion of feedback and evaluations — maintain high programme standards and provide insights for continuous improvement.
Additional Responsibilities
  • Share success stories and case studies to highlight best practices and contribute to team learning.
  • Collaborate with Operations or Product teams to improve workflows, systems, and data quality.
  • Participate in testing and documentation updates, helping refine processes for the During-Internship Team.
Contract & Compensation
When does this position start, and how does the contract work?

The role begins with a 3-month contract, designed to give both you and the team time to assess fit and performance. This initial period is a chance to demonstrate your impact and contribution.

At the end of the 3 months, there may be opportunities for long-term employment, based on your performance, alignment with our culture, and the evolving needs of the business. Many of our team members began on short-term contracts and transitioned into permanent roles after showing strong results and growth potential.

How is compensation structured for this role?

Being a PSA works best for those who thrive in people-based environments, as your compensation is directly tied to the primary KPIs: PSA Support Score (combined interns + host companies, measured monthly), Internship Completion Rate (measured monthly), and Internship Satisfaction Rate (measured quarterly). Alongside your base salary, you receive:

Monthly Commission — earned against the PSA Support Score and Internship Completion Rate, weighted 50/50, across three tiers: Meets Expectations (ME), Exceeds Expectations (EE), and Sets a New Standard (SNS). Paid the month following the performance period.

Quarterly Bonus — based on your Internship Satisfaction Rate over three months, reflecting the same ME/EE/SNS tiers. Paid the month after the quarter ends, provided you've completed the first three months.

In short: PSA compensation rewards consistent, high-quality support, successful internship completions, and strong satisfaction from both interns and host companies, with opportunities to earn more as performance exceeds expectations.

Daily Operations & Expectations
Who will I work with?

As a PSA, you are part of the During-Internship Team, led by Simone Labuschagne. Your role centres on collaboration and impact, connecting interns and host companies to ensure a seamless internship experience. You will work closely with:

  • Interns — provide guidance, support, and actionable feedback to help them thrive in a remote internship environment.
  • Host Companies — maintain strong, trust-based relationships, proactively address concerns, and ensure expectations are met.

Cross-Team Collaboration — your work extends beyond your core team:

  • Pre-Internship Team — Placement Matching Specialists ensure interns are paired with the right host companies; Placement Readiness Specialists prepare interns for interviews and manage scheduling.
  • Partnership Account Managers — support partner relationships and satisfaction.
  • Product Team — align on platform updates and optimise workflows.
  • Growth Team — collaborate on initiatives to expand our impact globally.
What does a typical week look like?

A successful PSA balances proactive monitoring, problem-solving, and reporting. Key activities include:

  • Monitoring feedback from interns and host companies to ensure timely insights.
  • Following up promptly to resolve gaps or issues.
  • Scheduling and documenting mediation or progress calls to address challenges constructively.
  • Reporting trends and insights to leadership, highlighting successes, risks, and opportunities for improvement.

In essence, PSAs act as the bridge between interns and host companies — catching issues early, facilitating productive communication, and creating a positive, outcome-driven internship experience.

What tools and technology will I be using?
  • Our proprietary platform called "Moo" for managing intern placements and company relationships.
  • HubSpot to organise your communications with companies and interns.
  • Slack for communication and collaboration.
  • Google Meet for virtual meetings.
  • LinkedIn to connect with your assigned host companies.
Support, Success & Growth
What support will I receive?
  • Comprehensive Onboarding & Training — led by Simone and experienced PSAs, covering WFL processes, communication frameworks, and conflict resolution/mediation best practices.
  • Ongoing Mentorship — regular check-ins and shared learning sessions ensure you continuously grow and refine your approach.
  • Practical Tools — templates, scripts, and SOPs designed to guide your work while allowing flexibility for your judgement and creativity.
What does success look like, and how is it measured?
  • Onboarding cadence is completed on schedule for every new placement, with accurate scoring and clean HubSpot logs.
  • After onboarding, your risk-tier calls are right — P1/P2 cases get caught and actioned before they escalate, and you're not spending time on placements that don't need it.
  • Interns and companies report high satisfaction and strong professional growth from start to finish.
  • Your data stays current throughout the placement, contributing reliably to team-level reporting without cleanup required from leadership.
  • You surface process improvements from real portfolio patterns, not just individual anecdotes.

What does great look like?
  • Curious First, Reactive Second — ask why at least twice before acting. Example: "What's really behind the slightly low score — workload, clarity, or relationship?"
  • Solutions with Follow-Through — share a clear action plan and check back, rather than "fire and forget." Great PSAs measure success by outcomes, not closed tickets.
  • Coach, Don't Just Fix — see interns and HCs as partners in growth, not just sources of problems. Shift language from "rules" to "opportunities for development."
  • Protect Relationships — every tough conversation is a chance to build trust. Even when escalation is needed, aim to leave both sides feeling heard and respected.
Working Life at Virtual Internships
What is the work culture like?

At Virtual Internships, our mission is to bridge the gap between education and the workplace, making internships accessible to all while preparing young professionals for the future of work. Every VI-er is expected to embrace a Culture of Growth — learning continuously, iterating on processes, and raising standards. Our culture encourages:

  • Continuous improvement — constantly refining processes to deliver better outcomes.
  • Innovation and calculated risk-taking — experimenting thoughtfully to drive impact.
  • Learning from challenges — treating setbacks as opportunities to grow.
  • Constructive feedback — sharing insights openly to support each other's development.
  • Collaboration and resource-sharing — working across teams to achieve shared goals.

We are ambitious and operationally rigorous, committed to helping team members develop professionally while fostering collaboration and connection across the organisation.

Key behaviours we value
  • Curiosity — a genuine interest in understanding the "why" behind every process.
  • Solution-oriented thinking — proactively identifying and acting on opportunities for improvement.
  • Comfort with ambiguity — adapting and making decisions in dynamic, fast-paced environments.

We are results-driven rather than schedule-driven. If you thrive in a flexible, high-impact environment and are motivated by growth and mission, you will feel at home here.

How does remote work function at VI?
As a fully remote team, we prioritise clear communication, trust, and connection. You'll structure your workday with flexibility while staying accountable for meetings and collaboration across time zones. We stay highly connected through tools like Slack, Google Meet, Google Workspace, HubSpot, and Gmail, ensuring alignment, transparency, and productivity across the team. Remote work at VI gives you autonomy, visibility, and the chance to make meaningful contributions from anywhere in the world.
Who We're Looking For
What kind of person thrives in this role?

You're energised by working within a clear system and by the parts of the job no system can automate — reading a tense conversation, knowing when to push and when to listen, rebuilding trust after a breakdown. You're comfortable with structure and consistency, but you don't hide behind process when a situation calls for a real, human response.

  • Reliable & Process-Minded: You execute a structured cadence consistently and log data accurately — this role depends on it.
  • Relationship-Focused: You value people over process within the framework, prioritising trust in every interaction.
  • Poised Under Pressure: You stay centred and constructive, especially with P1 cases and difficult conversations.
  • Emotionally Intelligent: You read between the lines and respond with empathy and boundaries.
  • Analytical & Proactive: You use scoring and risk data to spot root causes, not just symptoms.
  • Balanced & Fair: You advocate for interns and companies equally to create shared success.
Is this role not the right fit for me?

In the spirit of transparency, this role may not be the right fit if:

  • You prefer environments with low ambiguity, where priorities and next steps are always clearly defined.
  • You'd rather escalate a difficult conversation than have it yourself.
  • You find it hard to stay consistent with a structured cadence — weekly touchpoints, scoring, logging — once the novelty wears off.
  • You're looking for a role where most of your time goes to one-off tasks rather than long-running relationships you own end-to-end.
